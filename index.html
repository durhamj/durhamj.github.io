Igor Stravinsky

Russian Composer & Conductor (1882–1971)

Biography

Igor Fyodorovich Stravinsky (17 June 1882 – 6 April 1971) was a Russian composer and conductor widely considered one of the most important and influential composers of the 20th century. His music, particularly his ballets for the Ballets Russes, revolutionized music with innovations in rhythm, orchestration, and form.

Major Works

  • The Firebird (1910) – A ballet that blends Russian folklore with Stravinsky's distinctive style.
  • Petrushka (1911) – A ballet depicting the tragic love triangle of three puppets at a Russian fair.
  • The Rite of Spring (1913) – A groundbreaking ballet that famously caused a riot at its premiere due to its avant-garde nature.
  • Oedipus Rex (1927) – A neoclassical opera that integrates Greek mythology with Stravinsky's evolving style.
  • In Memoriam Dylan Thomas (1954) – A twelve-tone composition dedicated to the poet Dylan Thomas.

Compositional Periods

Russian Period (1913–1920)
